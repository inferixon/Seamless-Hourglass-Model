# SHG vs. Bullet Cluster

## Status

This note is a local clarification of how the Seamless Hourglass Model (SHG) could remain viable in the presence of the Bullet Cluster constraint.

It does **not** claim that SHG explains the Bullet Cluster. It defines the minimal route by which an SHG-like interpretation could survive it without collapsing into an arbitrary post-hoc dark-sector story.

---

## 1. The Bullet Cluster Constraint

The Bullet Cluster is a strong constraint on any model in which the additional gravitational component is assumed to be directly tied to visible baryonic matter.

In the Bullet Cluster, the dominant baryonic component is hot X-ray gas. During the cluster collision, this gas behaves as a collisional fluid: it is slowed, shock-heated, and spatially displaced from the galaxies. Gravitational lensing, however, indicates that the main lensing mass peaks remain closer to the collisionless galaxy components rather than to the gas.

This does not uniquely prove that the dark component must be a WIMP-like particle. It does, however, require that whatever produces the excess lensing behaves approximately as a collisionless component and is not simply locked to the local distribution of ordinary baryonic gas.

For SHG, the constraint can be stated as:

> A viable SHG extension must explain why the latent metric component follows persistent collisionless structure rather than the dominant collisional baryonic gas.

---

## 2. SHG Interpretation: Dark Matter as Latent Metric Scaffold

In SHG language, the observed baryonic world is not necessarily the whole gravitationally relevant state. It is the electromagnetically rendered subset of a broader matter-sector compression state.

The dark-matter-like component may therefore be interpreted as a **latent metric scaffold**:

> a gravitationally active but electromagnetically non-rendered component of the compression structure.

This scaffold is not assumed to be uncommitted baryonic matter, hidden ordinary gas, or a direct duplicate of visible matter. It is a non-rendered geometric remainder that affects the metric while lacking a direct electromagnetic interface.

A compact expression of the idea is:

```text
M_total_metric = M_EM_rendered + M_latent

M_EM_rendered  → baryonic matter visible through electromagnetic interaction
M_latent       → gravitationally active latent metric scaffold
```

This keeps SHG distinct from simple baryon-locked modified gravity. The dark component is not a local function of baryonic mass alone.

---

## 3. The Baryonization Coefficient Kb

A useful phenomenological parameter is the local baryonization coefficient:

```text
Kb(x, R) = M_latent(x, R) / M_EM_rendered(x, R)
```

Observationally, this can be approximated by:

```text
Kb(x, R) ≈ [M_lensing(x, R) - M_baryon(x, R)] / M_baryon(x, R)
```

where:

```text
M_baryon = M_stars + M_gas
```

The corresponding baryonized fraction is:

```text
β(x, R) = M_EM_rendered / M_total_metric
        = 1 / (1 + Kb)
```

At the cosmological average, the observed dark-to-baryonic matter ratio is approximately:

```text
Ωc / Ωb ≈ 5.3–5.4
```

In SHG terms, this may be read as a large-scale baseline for the ratio between metric-only structure and electromagnetically rendered matter. It should not be treated as a universal local constant. Local values of `Kb` may vary across galaxies, clusters, merger environments, and cosmic filaments.

---

## 4. Why the Gas Does Not Carry the Same Scaffold

The central question raised by the Bullet Cluster is:

> If the gas is baryonic and massive, why does the lensing scaffold not follow it?

The SHG answer is that the latent scaffold is not tied to baryonic mass as such. It is tied to **persistent phase-space structure**.

Hot intracluster gas is baryonic and electromagnetically rendered, but during a cluster collision it becomes highly dissipative. It is shock-heated, slowed by ram pressure, mixed, and driven into turbulent thermalized states. Its microscopic degrees of freedom are numerous, but they do not preserve the same macroscopic historical identity as the collisionless galaxy component.

In SHG terms:

```text
High microscopic entropy ≠ high scaffold persistence
```

The gas is fully visible in the electromagnetic runtime, but it is a poor anchor for latent metric structure because its collision history destroys coherent large-scale trajectory identity.

Galaxies and stellar components behave differently. During the merger, they are approximately collisionless. They retain phase-space persistence, trajectory continuity, and structural identity across the event. They therefore remain better anchors for the latent metric scaffold.

This suggests the following local SHG principle:

> The dark scaffold follows persistent, low-dissipation, phase-space-coherent structure rather than baryonic mass alone.

---

## 5. Scaffold Persistence Postulate

A possible SHG postulate compatible with the Bullet Cluster is:

> **Scaffold Persistence Postulate:**  
> The latent metric scaffold couples preferentially to persistent phase-space structures. Dissipative matter that loses historical identity through shock heating, turbulent mixing, and entropy production remains electromagnetically rendered but does not carry the same latent scaffold.

More formally, the latent component may depend on persistence variables rather than on baryonic density alone:

```text
M_latent ∝ P_persistence · C_phase · R_rigidity · D_low-dissipation
```

where:

```text
P_persistence      → preservation of large-scale trajectory identity
C_phase            → phase-space coherence
R_rigidity         → structural stability under collision
D_low-dissipation  → inverse dependence on shock heating, turbulence, and entropy production
```

This does not yet constitute a complete physical theory. It defines the direction in which SHG must be formalized if it is to survive the Bullet Cluster constraint.

---

## 6. Bullet Cluster in SHG Terms

Before the collision:

```text
Cluster A = galaxies_A + gas_A + scaffold_A
Cluster B = galaxies_B + gas_B + scaffold_B
```

After the collision:

```text
galaxies_A/B  → pass through approximately collisionlessly
scaffold_A/B  → remains associated with persistent collisionless structure
gas_A/B       → shocks, slows, mixes, and loses prior large-scale identity
```

The lensing peaks therefore remain near the galaxy components, while the X-ray gas is displaced.

Under this interpretation, the Bullet Cluster does not kill SHG automatically. It kills only the naive version in which the latent gravitational component is expected to follow total baryonic mass directly.

---

## 7. Observational Program

The SHG-compatible test program would not ask merely whether dark matter exists. It would ask whether the inferred latent metric component correlates better with phase-space persistence than with baryonic mass.

A minimal program:

```text
1. Build lensing mass maps.
2. Build baryonic mass maps:
   - stellar mass
   - hot gas mass
3. Compute local Kb maps:
   Kb(x, R) ≈ [M_lensing - M_baryon] / M_baryon
4. Compare Kb with:
   - stellar/collisionless distribution
   - X-ray gas distribution
   - gas entropy
   - shock fronts
   - turbulence indicators
   - temperature maps
   - merger history
   - phase-space coherence proxies
5. Test whether Kb is better predicted by persistence variables
   than by baryonic density alone.
```

A successful SHG extension should predict that high-dissipation gas regions have lower scaffold association per unit baryonic mass than persistent collisionless components.

---

## 8. Failure Conditions

This SHG branch should be considered weakened or rejected if:

```text
1. Kb cannot be defined independently of the data it is meant to explain.
2. The scaffold can be arbitrarily placed wherever lensing requires it.
3. Kb shows no robust relation to phase-space persistence, dissipation, or merger history.
4. Excess lensing consistently follows collisional gas rather than collisionless structure.
5. The model can explain every possible lensing configuration without losing confidence.
```

The important requirement is local falsifiability. SHG does not need a single observation to destroy its whole ontology, but specific branches must be allowed to fail.

---

## 9. Summary

The Bullet Cluster does not force SHG to become a WIMP model, but it does force SHG to abandon any simple baryon-locked account of dark matter.

A viable SHG interpretation must treat the dark component as a latent metric scaffold associated with persistent, low-dissipation phase-space structure. The hot gas is visible and baryonic, but it is not a strong scaffold anchor because it becomes shock-heated, turbulent, and historically decohered during the merger.

The useful phenomenological parameter is:

```text
Kb = M_latent / M_EM_rendered
```

In this interpretation, `Kb` measures the local render gap between gravitationally active structure and electromagnetically rendered matter.

Thus, the Bullet Cluster becomes not a direct death sentence for SHG, but a sharp constraint:

> SHG survives only if the latent metric scaffold follows persistence, not baryonic mass alone.
